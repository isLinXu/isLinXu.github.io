# Accomplishments

This page collects representative outcomes across research, engineering, open-source contribution, and public knowledge curation.

## Research and Evaluation Highlights

- MME related work: Xu Lin is listed among the authors of the MME benchmark, making multimodal evaluation one of the clearest paper-related signals in the public profile
- YOLO-Master direction: current public material shows an active detection line connected to YOLO-style architectures and efficiency-oriented design
- Public resume material also points to work on multimodal evaluation, large-scale image-text data construction, and practical recall optimization

## Open-Source and Engineering Highlights

- [LlamaFactory](https://github.com/isLinXu/LlamaFactory): a visible fork-based contribution direction in efficient fine-tuning, LLM, and VLM engineering
- [paper-list](https://github.com/isLinXu/paper-list): a visible public repository combining paper collection, automation, and open publishing workflow
- [crablet](https://github.com/isLinXu/crablet): practical AI assistant and agentic system building
- [YOLOv8_Efficient](https://github.com/isLinXu/YOLOv8_Efficient): long-running practical computer vision engineering work
- Agent-oriented repositories such as [darwin-agent](https://github.com/isLinXu/darwin-agent) and [eval-skills](https://github.com/isLinXu/eval-skills) show a clear current shift toward agent systems

## Public Curation and Knowledge Impact

- [paper-list](https://github.com/isLinXu/paper-list): a flagship paper curation and update system
- [awesome-road-map](https://github.com/isLinXu/awesome-road-map): structured roadmap-oriented public curation
- [awesome-foundation-agents](https://github.com/isLinXu/awesome-foundation-agents): foundation-agent oriented awesome-style collection
- [VALSE-WorkShop](https://github.com/isLinXu/VALSE-WorkShop): a long-running public aggregation of workshop and resource material
- [paper-read-notes](https://github.com/isLinXu/paper-read-notes), [Stanford-CS-Course](https://github.com/isLinXu/Stanford-CS-Course), and [prompt-engineering-note](https://github.com/isLinXu/prompt-engineering-note) show a sustained habit of learning in public and turning study into reusable public assets

## Why These Highlights Matter

Taken together, these highlights show a profile that is not limited to a single paper, repository, or benchmark. A recurring pattern is the ability to connect:

- research with evaluation
- evaluation with deployment
- deployment with reusable engineering
- engineering with public documentation and open curation

That combination is one of the clearest patterns in the portfolio.
